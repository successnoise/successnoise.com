Sulphur

Sulphur is a chemical element with the symbol S and atomic number 16. It is abundant, multivalent and nonmetallic. Under normal conditions, sulfur atoms form cyclic octatomic molecules with a chemical formula S8. Elemental sulfur is a bright yellow, crystalline solid at room temperature.


Interesting Facts :

→ Sulphur compounds have strong smell but the pure element is odorless.
→ Sulphur is the UK spelling whereas sulfur is the US spelling.
