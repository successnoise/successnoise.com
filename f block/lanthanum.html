Lanthanum

Lanthanum is a chemical element with the symbol La and atomic number 57. It is a soft, ductile, silvery-white metal that tarnishes slowly when exposed to air. It is the eponym of the lanthanide series, a group of 15 similar elements between lanthanum and lutetium in the periodic table, of which lanthanum is the first and the prototype. Lanthanum is traditionally counted among the rare earth elements.


Interesting Facts :

→ It is used to reduce blood phosphate levels in people with kidney disease.
→ According to IUPAC, lanthanum is a f block element but some organisation put it in the category of d block.
