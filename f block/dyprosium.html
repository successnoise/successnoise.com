Dyprosium

Dysprosium is the chemical element with the symbol Dy and atomic number 66. It is a rare-earth element in the lanthanide series with a metallic silver luster. Dysprosium is never found in nature as a free element, though, like other lanthanides, it is found in various minerals, such as xenotime.


Interesting Facts :

→ Word origin: From dysprositos, which means “difficult to get at” in Greek.
→ Paul Lecoq de Boisbaudran identified dysprosium in 1886, but it wasn't isolated as a pure metal until the 1950s by Frank Spedding.
