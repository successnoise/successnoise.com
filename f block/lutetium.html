Lutetium

Lutetium is a chemical element with the symbol Lu and atomic number 71. It is a silvery white metal, which resists corrosion in dry air, but not in moist air. Lutetium is the last element in the lanthanide series, and it is traditionally counted among the rare earths.


Interesting Facts :

→ Its compounds are used as hosts for scintillators and X-ray phosphors, and the oxide is used in optical lenses.
→ The element behaves as a typical rare earth, forming a series of compounds in oxidation state +3, such as lutetium sesquioxide, sulfate, and chloride.
