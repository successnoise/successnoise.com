Rhodium

Rhodium is a chemical element with the symbol Rh and atomic number 45. It is a very rare, silvery-white, hard, corrosion-resistant, and chemically inert transition metal. It is a noble metal and a member of the platinum group. It has only one naturally occurring isotope: ¹⁰³Rh.


Interesting Facts :

→ Named after the Greek word for rose, “rhodon,” rhodium is the rarest metal in the platinum group.
→ It has a lower density and higher melting point than platinum, making it one of the toughest precious metals to melt.
